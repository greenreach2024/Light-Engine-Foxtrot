Safety & Compliance

GreenReach is committed to food safety, driver safety, and full regulatory compliance under Ontario and Canadian law.

Food Safety Training

All drivers complete GreenReach food safety onboarding, aligned with Ontario's Health Protection and Promotion Act.

  • Temperature monitoring for perishable goods (2–4°C cold chain)
  • Proper handling of organic and conventionally grown produce
  • Allergen identification and customer communication protocols
  • Hygiene and handwashing procedures before and during shifts

Vehicle Safety Standards

We require vehicles meet Ontario's Highway Traffic Act standards and maintain safe conditions for food transport.

  • Annual vehicle safety inspection (Safety Standards Certificate for motor vehicles)
  • Clean and sanitized cargo area — free of chemicals, pets, and contaminants
  • Insulated delivery bags provided by GreenReach to maintain produce quality
  • Seasonal readiness — winter tires required November through April

Insurance & Background Checks

All drivers must carry proper insurance and consent to a comprehensive background screening.

  • Valid auto insurance with minimum $1M liability coverage (Ontario requirement)
  • Criminal Record Check (CRC) through an accredited Canadian police service
  • Driving abstract reviewed — no DUI/DWI, reckless driving, or licence suspensions
  • Commercial use endorsement on insurance policy (if required by insurer)

Regulatory Compliance

GreenReach operates in full compliance with applicable federal and provincial regulations.

  • Ontario Food Premises Regulation (O. Reg. 493/17) — transport standards
  • Canada's Safe Food for Canadians Act (SFCA) — for interprovincial deliveries
  • WHMIS awareness for any cleaning/sanitizing agents used in vehicle maintenance
  • Independent contractor relationship — drivers manage their own tax obligations (GST/HST if applicable)

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I earn delivering with GreenReach?

Earnings vary based on number of deliveries, distance, and tips. Drivers earn a base rate per delivery plus 100% of customer tips. Most active drivers in the Kingston area earn competitive rates comparable to other delivery platforms, with shorter average delivery distances.

Do I need prior delivery experience?

No. We provide comprehensive onboarding including food safety training, app orientation, and delivery best practices. Prior experience is welcomed but not required — what matters most is reliability and a commitment to quality service.

Can I deliver on a bicycle or e-bike?

Yes! We support bicycle and e-bike deliveries for orders within shorter distances. You'll need an insulated delivery bag (provided by GreenReach) and the ability to carry produce boxes safely. No driver's licence is needed for non-motorized vehicles.

What is the background check process?

We request a Criminal Record Check (CRC) through an accredited Canadian police service and review your driving abstract. This process typically takes 5–10 business days. We cover the cost of the background check for approved applicants.

What food safety training is required?

All drivers complete a GreenReach food safety orientation (approximately 1 hour online). If you don't already have a Safe Food Handling certificate, you'll have 30 days after approval to complete one through an Ontario-recognized provider. GreenReach provides resources and can reimburse the certification cost.

What areas does GreenReach deliver to?

We currently serve Kingston and surrounding areas within approximately 50 km of our network farms. This includes downtown Kingston, the west end, east end, Cataraqui, Bath, Gananoque, and other nearby communities. Coverage areas are expanding as we grow.

How long does the application process take?

Most applications are reviewed within 3–5 business days. Once approved, you can complete online onboarding and start delivering within a week. The background check may add an additional 5–10 business days depending on processing times.
