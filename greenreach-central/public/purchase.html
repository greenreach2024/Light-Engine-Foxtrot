Why growers choose Light Engine

Efficient. Consistent. Flexible.

Every element of your grow — climate, light, water, nutrients, labour — managed against the plant, not the clock. More pounds per kilowatt. Fewer surprises.

⚡

Greater growing efficiency

Real-time VPD, PPFD, and DLI targeting dims lights, trims irrigation, and stages HVAC so you hit yield targets on less energy and less water — measured, not estimated.

◎

Improved consistency

Every seeding follows a versioned recipe. Every deviation is logged. Every harvest lands within a tight quality band — so buyers can count on what shows up each week.

⇌

Dynamic environment management

Zone-by-zone control lets one grow room run leafy greens, herbs, and microgreens at the same time — each crop on its own light, climate, and irrigation profile.

Multi-crop by design

One room. Many crops. No compromises.

Traditional rooms are locked to a single recipe. Light Engine splits a room into independent zones — lettuce next to basil next to microgreens — each with its own spectrum, photoperiod, VPD target, and irrigation schedule.

  • Per-zone light, climate, and irrigation profiles
  • Crop-aware scheduling: seed, transplant, and harvest dates computed from targets
  • Switch a zone from basil to microgreens in a single click

Hands-on — Seed to table

Evie

Evie is the grower assistant every farmer wishes they had. She watches your rooms around the clock, nudges your environment before stress shows up in the plants, and turns your crop plan into a clear weekly rhythm: what to seed, what to transplant, what to harvest — and when.

  • Tunes VPD, light, and irrigation against live plant targets
  • Generates seeding, transplant, and harvest schedules automatically
  • Sends plain-English alerts to the team before yield is at risk
  • Hands off cleanly to humans — no black boxes, every action is logged

Research — Sustainable food systems

Gwen

Gwen is pure R&D. She collaborates with a global network of researchers, universities, and institutions to push the boundary of sustainable food production — then quietly pulls those findings back into your rooms as better recipes, better crop templates, and better defaults.

  • Designs multi-site trials and compares outcomes across farms
  • Curates crop templates fed by peer research and your own telemetry
  • Publishes findings back to the global network with proper attribution
  • Keeps your farm aligned with the state of the art, not the state of 2015
