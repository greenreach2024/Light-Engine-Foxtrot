N Nutrient System & Water Quality

Reservoir & Dosing Critical

  • Check reservoir water level — refill with RO/filtered water if below operating minimum
    Target: maintain 75–100% capacity to prevent pump cavitation
  • Measure and record pH — target range 5.5–6.2 for most leafy greens
    pH drift >0.5 from last reading warrants investigation
  • Measure and record EC/TDS — target EC 1.2–2.4 mS/cm depending on crop stage
    Seedlings: 0.8–1.2 | Vegetative: 1.4–2.0 | Mature: 1.8–2.4
  • Check water temperature — target 65–72°F (18–22°C)
    Above 75°F risks Pythium; below 60°F slows nutrient uptake
  • Verify auto-doser operation — confirm pumps are cycling, no air locks
    Check acid, base, and nutrient concentrate reservoirs are above min levels
  • Inspect misting nozzles for clogs — check spray pattern on 3–5 random nozzles
    Clogged nozzles = dry roots = crop loss within hours in aeroponics
  • Check misting cycle timer — verify on/off intervals match crop prescription
    Typical: 5 sec on / 3–5 min off (high-pressure); 30 sec on / 2 min off (low-pressure)
C Environment & Climate

Environmental Readings Critical

  • Record ambient temperature per zone — target 68–77°F (20–25°C) day / 60–68°F (16–20°C) night
    Temperature differential (DIF) of 5–10°F promotes strong stem growth
  • Record relative humidity per zone — target 50–70% RH
    Above 80% RH increases powdery mildew & botrytis risk; below 40% causes tip burn
  • Calculate and record VPD — target 0.8–1.2 kPa for vegetative, 1.0–1.4 kPa for fruiting
    VPD outside range = stomatal stress → reduced transpiration and growth
  • Check CO₂ levels if supplementing — target 800–1200 ppm during lights-on
    Ambient ~420 ppm; supplement only when lights are on and ventilation is reduced
  • Verify HVAC and dehumidifier operation — listen for unusual sounds, check drain lines
    Condensate drain blockages cause overflow and humidity control failure
  • Check air circulation fans — confirm all oscillating fans operational, no dead zones
    Stagnant air pockets = localized high humidity = disease hotspots
L Lighting

Light System Check Important

  • Verify all LED fixtures are on during scheduled photoperiod
    A single failed fixture creates uneven canopy and wasted grow slots
  • Check light spectrum & intensity settings match current growth stage
    PPFD targets: seedling 150–250 | vegetative 250–400 | finishing 400–600 µmol/m²/s
  • Confirm photoperiod schedule — verify timer/controller on-off times
    Typical leafy greens: 16h on / 8h off; microgreens: 12–16h; herbs: 14–18h
  • Spot-check canopy distance — adjust fixture height if plants growing into lights
    Maintain 6–12" clearance depending on fixture type and crop
P Crop Health & Production

Visual Crop Inspection Critical

  • Walk each zone — inspect canopy for wilting, discoloration, pest damage, or tip burn
    Catch problems within 24h: early intervention saves entire batches
  • Check root health on 5–10 random plants — roots should be white, firm, abundant
    Brown/slimy roots = root rot (Pythium). Isolate and treat immediately.
  • Monitor for pests — inspect undersides of leaves (aphids, thrips, whitefly, fungus gnats)
    Use sticky traps as early-warning indicators; check traps daily
  • Remove dead/diseased plant material — do not leave debris in grow area
    Dead material = pathogen reservoir. Bag and remove from growing environment.
  • Record germination rates for newly seeded trays (within 3 days of sowing)
    Target >90% germination; below 80% indicates seed quality or environment issue

Seeding & Harvesting Routine

  • Seed scheduled trays per production plan — record variety, lot #, cell count, date
    Pre-soak seeds if required by variety (basil, cilantro benefit from 4–8h soak)
  • Transplant seedlings at proper stage — typically 2–3 true leaves or 7–14 days
    Minimize root disturbance during transplant; handle by plug, not stem
  • Harvest mature crops per schedule — harvest in early morning or with lights recently on
    Harvest to cold storage within 15 min; target 34–38°F post-harvest temp
  • Weigh and record harvest yields — log by variety, zone, tray, and days to harvest
    Track yield trends to identify declining performance zones
  • Clean and sanitize harvested tray positions — prep for next planting
    Use food-safe sanitizer (e.g., hydrogen peroxide 3%, or StarSan)
O Operations & Safety

Facility Check Routine

  • Check for water leaks — inspect floor drains, fittings, reservoir overflow
    Even small leaks cause humidity spikes and slip hazards
  • Verify backup systems — confirm UPS/generator ready, backup pump accessible
    Aeroponic roots dry out in 20–30 min without misting; backup is critical
  • Review sensor dashboard alerts — acknowledge or resolve any IoT alarms
    Check Light Engine dashboard for anomaly flags and sensor offline alerts
  • Update daily log — summarize observations, actions taken, and any issues noted
    Best practice: timestamped digital log accessible to all team members

W Water System Maintenance

Filtration & Plumbing Critical

  • Rinse inline sediment filters (50–100 micron pre-filters)
    Rinse under clean water until flow is clear; replace if media is degraded
  • Clean misting nozzle screens — soak in vinegar or citric acid solution (15 min)
    For high-pressure aeroponics: disassemble, soak, and reassemble 5–10 nozzles per week on rotation
  • Inspect high-pressure pump — check pressure gauge, listen for cavitation noise
    Target operating pressure: 80–100 PSI (high-pressure aero). Log readings.
  • Check accumulator/pressure tank pre-charge (if equipped)
    Pre-charge should be ~10 PSI below cut-in pressure
  • Flush nutrient lines — run clean water through manifolds for 5 min
    Prevents salt buildup inside supply lines and manifolds
  • Inspect all tubing connections and clamps for drips or mineral deposits
T Water Quality Testing

Lab & In-House Testing Important

  • Full nutrient solution analysis — test N, P, K, Ca, Mg, S, Fe, Mn, Zn, B, Cu, Mo
    Use an in-house multi-ion meter or send to lab. Compare against target recipe.
  • Test source water quality — pH, EC, alkalinity, hardness
    Source water changes seasonally; high alkalinity buffers pH adjustment
  • Dissolved oxygen check — target >6 mg/L in nutrient solution
    Low DO in warm water promotes root pathogens. Consider adding air stones or chillers.
  • Check for biofilm in reservoir — visually inspect walls and bottom for slime
    Biofilm harbors pathogens. Clean and treat with H₂O₂ if present.
E Equipment & Environment

HVAC & Air Quality Important

  • Inspect and rinse HVAC return air pre-filters
    Dust-loaded pre-filters reduce airflow 20–40% and strain compressors
  • Check dehumidifier drain pans and condensate pumps
    Standing water in drain pans grows mold and bacteria
  • Verify exhaust fan operation and intake damper position
    Negative pressure prevents contaminants from entering; check with smoke pencil
  • Wipe down LED fixture lenses with damp microfiber — dust reduces PAR output
    Even thin dust layers can reduce light transmission 10–15%
  • Inspect cable management and electrical connections — check for moisture intrusion
    High-humidity grows corrode terminals; spray with dielectric grease if needed

IPM & Sanitation Important

  • Replace sticky traps — count and log captures by pest type
    Tracking trends: spikes in counts = emerging infestation. Act before visual symptoms.
  • Apply preventive biological controls if part of IPM program
    Beneficial insects (e.g., Stratiolaelaps for fungus gnats, Amblyseius for thrips)
  • Sanitize walkways, work surfaces, and harvesting tools
    Quaternary ammonium or dilute bleach (200 ppm). Let air dry.
  • Launder or replace grow room shoe covers / dedicated footwear
    Footwear is the #1 vector for introducing pathogens and pests
  • Inspect door seals and pest exclusion screens on intake vents

Production Planning Routine

  • Review production schedule — confirm seedling pipeline matches demand forecast
    Adjust seed quantities based on germination rates and order book
  • Inventory consumables — seeds, grow media, nutrients, packaging, labels
    Reorder anything below 2-week supply threshold
  • Review yield data and compare to targets — identify underperforming zones or crops
    Use Light Engine farm inventory dashboard for trend analysis
  • Back up farm data — sensor logs, recipes, group configurations
    Verify GreenReach Central sync is current; check edge device backup status

W Deep Water System Service

Reservoir & Line Cleaning Critical

  • Full reservoir drain, scrub, and sanitize — use H₂O₂ (35% diluted to 3%) or hypochlorous acid
    Drain completely, scrub walls/bottom, rinse twice, refill with fresh solution
  • Replace inline sediment filters (50–100 micron)
    Even if rinsed weekly, media degrades. Replace monthly for consistent flow.
  • Clean or replace UV sterilizer sleeve (if equipped)
    Mineral deposits on quartz sleeve reduce UV-C transmission to ineffective levels
  • Inspect and clean float valves and level sensors
    Mineral scale on float valves causes stuck-open or stuck-closed failures
  • Flush the entire aeroponic manifold system with enzyme cleaner or H₂O₂ solution
    Run cleaning solution through all misting lines for 15 min, then flush with clean water
C Probe Calibration & Sensors

Calibration Schedule Critical

  • Calibrate pH probe — 2-point calibration with pH 4.0 and 7.0 buffers
    Rinse probe with distilled water between buffers. Replace probe if slope <80% or offset >±30mV.
  • Calibrate EC/TDS probe — use certified 1.413 mS/cm standard
    Rinse probe thoroughly before and after. Check cell constant if readings drift.
  • Verify temperature sensor accuracy — cross-check against a NIST-traceable reference thermometer
    ±0.5°C tolerance acceptable; recalibrate or replace if >1°C deviation
  • Test dissolved oxygen sensor (if equipped) — calibrate in air-saturated water
    Replace membrane cap if response time >60 sec or readings drift >0.5 mg/L
  • Verify humidity sensor accuracy — compare against sling psychrometer or reference
    RH sensors drift over time; ±3% RH acceptable. Replace if >5% deviation.
  • Check and calibrate auto-doser peristaltic pump flow rates
    Run cal routine: pump 100mL, compare actual vs. expected. Adjust if >5% off.
E Equipment Deep Service

Mechanical Systems Important

  • Inspect high-pressure pump seals and diaphragm — check for leaks or pressure drop
    Replace seals preventively every 6–12 months depending on run hours
  • Clean or replace HVAC coil filters
    Dirty coils reduce efficiency 15–25% and can freeze up in cooling mode
  • Deep-clean dehumidifier coils with coil cleaner and rinse
    Scale and biofilm on coils reduces dehumidification capacity
  • Inspect wiring and connections on all grow room controllers
    Look for corrosion, loose terminals, and damaged insulation
  • Lubricate fan motors and bearings as specified by manufacturer
    Use recommended lubricant type; over-lubrication attracts dust
  • Test emergency shutoff and alarm systems
    Simulate failure conditions: power loss, high-temp, flood sensor, smoke detector
Q Plant Science & QA

Tissue Sampling & Analysis Best Practice

  • Collect tissue samples from 2–3 representative crop varieties
    Sample recently mature leaves (3rd–5th from growing tip). Minimum 50g fresh weight per sample.
  • Send tissue samples to certified lab for macro & micronutrient analysis
    Compare results against published sufficiency ranges for each crop species
  • Review lab results and adjust nutrient formula if needed
    Common findings: Ca or Mg deficiency in fast-growing crops; Fe or Mn lockout at high pH
  • Conduct food safety swab test on harvest surfaces and packaging area
    Test for total plate count, coliforms, E.coli, Listeria. Required for GAP certification.
  • Review pest pressure trends — adjust IPM strategy based on trap counts and scouting
    If biological controls are insufficient, evaluate targeted organic-approved treatments

Business & Records Routine

  • Review yield per square foot by crop — compare to benchmarks
    Leafy greens: 1–3 lbs/sq ft/month; microgreens: 2–5 lbs/sq ft/month
  • Calculate cost per unit (seed-to-harvest) — track against revenue per unit
    Include: seed, nutrients, energy, labor, packaging, delivery
  • Update crop pricing based on cost analysis and market conditions
    Use Light Engine Crop Pricing page; ensure all 50 recipe crops have current pricing
  • Review energy consumption — compare kWh to production output
    Target: <50 kWh/kg for leafy greens with LED; optimize DLI to reduce waste

W Major Water System Overhaul

Deep System Service Critical

  • Replace all misting nozzles (or at minimum 50% on rotation)
    Nozzle orifices wear over time, changing droplet size and coverage pattern
  • Replace peristaltic pump tubing on all dosers
    Tubing fatigues and loses elasticity, causing inaccurate dosing (±20% drift)
  • Replace RO membrane pre-filters and carbon block (if using RO system)
    Replace sediment pre-filter every 3 months; carbon block every 6–12 months
  • Test RO membrane rejection rate — should be >95% TDS rejection
    If rejection <90%, membrane needs replacement ($100–300 depending on size)
  • Deep-clean all grow channels, towers, or aeroponic chambers
    Disassemble accessible sections. Soak in enzyme cleaner → scrub → H₂O₂ rinse → reassemble
  • Pressure test all plumbing lines — check for slow leaks at joints
    Cap discharge end, pressurize to 120% operating pressure, hold 15 min, check gauge
S Sensor & Probe Replacement

Probe Lifecycle Management Important

  • Evaluate pH probe lifespan — replace if >12 months old or calibration slope <75%
    Budget 2–4 pH probes/year for continuous immersion applications
  • Evaluate EC probe — replace if readings are unstable after calibration
    EC probes last longer than pH but platinized electrodes degrade with use
  • Replace PAR/PPFD sensor batteries and verify calibration
    Photodiode sensors drift 2–5% per year; factory recalibration recommended annually
  • Verify IoT sensor firmware is up to date — apply updates if available
    Check SwitchBot, environmental sensor, and controller firmware versions
F Facility & Infrastructure

HEPA Filters & Air Handling Critical

  • Replace HEPA filters on intake air handlers
    HEPA filters (H13/H14) should be replaced every 3–6 months in dusty environments
  • Replace carbon/activated charcoal filters (if used for odor control)
    Carbon exhausts after 3–6 months depending on load; replace when breakthrough detected
  • Professional HVAC service — refrigerant check, coil cleaning, electrical inspection
    Schedule with HVAC contractor; verify refrigerant charge and compressor amps
  • Inspect and clean all ductwork — check for mold, dust buildup, or condensation
    Ductwork mold = airborne spore distribution throughout grow. Critical to remediate.

Structural & Safety Important

  • Inspect grow rack structural integrity — check for rust, loose bolts, bent members
    Water and nutrient exposure accelerates corrosion on steel racks
  • Test all GFI/GFCI outlets — press test button, verify trip, reset
    Water + electricity = critical safety concern. Replace any non-functioning GFCIs immediately.
  • Inspect fire suppression system — check extinguisher dates, smoke detectors, sprinklers
    Many jurisdictions require annual professional inspection
  • Review and update SOPs — incorporate lessons learned from past quarter
    New crop recipes, pest encounters, equipment failures should update procedures
  • Conduct full inventory audit — reconcile physical stock vs. system records
    Seeds, nutrients, packaging, spare parts. Update Light Engine inventory counts.

Water Quality Lab Panel Best Practice

  • Send comprehensive water sample to accredited lab — full mineral panel + microbiology
    Test for: heavy metals (Pb, Cd, As, Hg), total coliforms, Legionella, algae counts
  • Test source water for chloramine/chlorine residual if on municipal supply
    Chloramine is not removed by standard carbon filters; requires catalytic carbon or UV
  • Compare lab results against historical baseline — flag any trending contaminants
    Keep a running spreadsheet of all quarterly lab results for trend analysis

B Aeroponic System Best Practices
Aeroponics Key Principle: Roots are suspended in air and misted with nutrient solution. There is zero buffer — if the misting system fails, roots desiccate within 20–30 minutes. Redundancy and monitoring are not optional, they are essential.

Water & Nutrient Management

  • Maintain sterile reservoir conditions. Use beneficial microbes (Bacillus subtilis) OR chemical sterilization (H₂O₂, hypochlorous acid) — never both simultaneously. Choose one strategy.
  • Change nutrient solution fully every 1–2 weeks. Top-offs cause mineral ratio drift. EC may read correct while Ca:Mg or N:K ratios are skewed. Full change resets ratios.
  • Use RO or distilled water as your base. Starting from near-zero EC gives full control over nutrient composition. Tap water adds unknown variables (chlorine, carbonates, sodium).
  • Monitor root zone temperature separately from air temp. In aeroponics, mist temp = root temp. Chill nutrient solution to 65–68°F in warm environments.
  • Target droplet size of 20–50 microns for true high-pressure aeroponics. Larger droplets (>100µm) behave more like NFT and reduce oxygen exposure to roots. Use quality solenoids and nozzles rated for the target micron range.

Crop Management

  • Succession planting is mandatory. Stagger seeding so you never have all trays at the same growth stage. Aim for daily or every-other-day seeding to ensure continuous harvest.
  • Track Days to Harvest (DTH) per variety per zone. DTH varies with light, temperature, and nutrient programs. Use Light Engine grow plans to dial in optimal schedules per crop.
  • Tissue analysis is the gold standard for nutrition. Visual symptoms appear when deficiency is already severe. Monthly tissue samples catch sub-clinical issues 2–3 weeks before visible damage.
  • Implement FIFO (First In, First Out) for all harvested product. Label every container with harvest date, variety, and zone. Cold chain must hold 34–38°F from harvest to customer.
  • Keep detailed seed lot records. Track germination rates per seed lot. If germination drops below 85%, contact supplier or switch lots. Poor germination wastes tray slots and disrupts production planning.

IPM (Integrated Pest Management)

  • Prevention > Treatment. Sealed grow rooms, HEPA-filtered intake, positive/negative pressure, foot baths at entry, and dedicated grow room clothing prevent 90% of pest introductions.
  • Yellow sticky traps at every door and air intake. Check daily. They are early-warning systems, not control measures. 1 trap per 100 sq ft minimum.
  • Quarantine all new plant material for 7 days. Never introduce outside plants or cuttings directly into the grow room. Inspect under magnification before integration.
  • Maintain beneficial insect populations preventively. Sachets of predatory mites (Amblyseius cucumeris, A. swirskii) provide ongoing protection without chemical residue.
  • If treating, rotate chemical classes. Never use the same mode of action twice in a row. Resistance develops rapidly in controlled environments where populations are dense.

Facility & Energy

  • Optimize DLI (Daily Light Integral) not just PPFD. A shorter photoperiod at higher intensity can achieve the same DLI as a longer one at lower intensity, but energy cost differs. Calculate: DLI = PPFD × hours × 0.0036.
  • Insulate grow rooms from exterior walls. Thermal bridging causes condensation → mold. R-13 minimum insulation on exterior walls and ceiling.
  • Maintain slight negative pressure in grow rooms. This prevents unfiltered air (and pests) from entering through cracks and doors. Verify with differential pressure gauge or smoke test.
  • Install leak detection sensors at floor level. Water damage is the #1 facility insurance claim for indoor farms. $20 sensors can prevent $20,000 in damage.
  • Document everything. Environmental data, nutrient recipes, pest observations, maintenance actions, and yield data should all be logged digitally. Use Light Engine's dashboard, not notebooks, for searchable, trend-able records.
